The submission, as six questions. Each opens.

1. What is the goal?

The competition asked for epistemic tooling on hard contested questions, its three named cases being pandemic origins, black holes, and eggs: turn messy sources into typed claims tied to their origins, make the relations among claims legible, tell a reader what to believe and what to look at next, scale with better AI, and compound across people and teams. This submission reads all of that as one goal: knowledge that survives attenuation. A claim counts as knowledge when it still stands after trust in whoever produced it is dialed down toward zero, and work compounds exactly when communities' claims compose without importing each other's authority.

Doors: the minimum constitution; the criteria map.

2. What has been performing this function?

The internet, in three configurations, carrying claims on a substrate that sees only packets. A cooperative kernel where social accountability supplied the verification; the same contracts meeting an adversarial population; a patched kernel where authorities compensate for standing that does not recompute. Each era, encoded as a kernel and run through the protocol's conformance checker, violates exactly the invariants the contracts fix, with each violation mapped to a documented failure, so the recentralization is derived rather than asserted. The same trajectory is now beginning one layer up, over claims themselves.

Doors: the counterexample exhibit; the constitution's internet section.

3. Given recursive modelers, how does it need to be shaped?

The history above is what recursive modelers do to fixed trust signals: model them, aim at them, and manufacture them cheaper than the substance they once certified. The division that survives this: fix the little that composition and adversaries require, as contracts, and leave every semantic judgment free, as parameters, because semantic controls spend the exact variety communities need for judging.

Doors: the contract register; the asymmetric weapon.

4. What is the shape?

Eleven fixed contracts and a gate. Claims are typed and content-addressed; a grade recomputes from the public graph; an import grounds nothing until a local fork types it; views move no grade. Everything else, the type systems, the standing rules, the forums, the licenses, is a free parameter, and a boundary linter holds the two registers disjoint.

Doors: the contract register; the math kernel.

5. What else has been shaped like this?

Every mature institution runs these mechanisms by hand: precedent chains in common law, dependency graphs in software, replication in science, load orders in game modding, slashing in decentralized finance. The lineage corpus grounds that record and one conjecture: nothing yet composes all five axes for empirical and contested knowledge.

Door: the lineage corpus.

6. What is the shape, running?

This app. A feed over typed claims, every grade computed on your device from the public graph, the ranking set by you and moving no grade. The repository's checks rerun in about two minutes, and the substrate is pinned to the protocol by commit and hash.

Doors: Open the app; the repository.

Grades are computed on device from the public graph. A grade is a computed reading, never a claim of truth.
